Nikola Tesla

The Master of Lightning

Revolutionary Inventions

Tesla Coil (1891)

A revolutionary high-voltage transformer circuit that would later power radio technology and modern wireless transmission. Capable of producing millions of volts of electricity and creating spectacular lightning displays.

High Voltage Transformer

AC Power System (1888)

The alternating current electrical system that powers our world today. Tesla's most profound contribution to humanity, enabling long-distance power transmission and the modern electrical grid.

Power Distribution

Wireless Transmission (1893)

Pioneered the transmission of energy through the air, laying groundwork for radio, WiFi, and modern wireless communications. The Wardenclyffe Tower was his ambitious attempt at worldwide wireless power.

Wireless Technology

The Mad Scientist's Laboratory

Tesla's laboratory at Colorado Springs was a temple of scientific innovation. Here, he produced artificial lightning bolts up to 135 feet long, illuminated light bulbs from miles away, and conducted his most audacious experiments.

Wireless Light

First demonstration of wireless lighting systems

Radio Signals

Detected signals from outer space

Tesla's Vision

The Tesla Philosophy

Free Energy

Tesla's dream was to provide free wireless energy to the world, believing that access to power was a fundamental human right.

Scientific Method

He visualized his inventions completely in his mind before building them, claiming he could even test them mentally.

Future Vision

Tesla predicted smartphones, wireless internet, and autonomous vehicles decades before their invention.
